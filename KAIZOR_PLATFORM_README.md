# KAIZOR Platform

KAIZOR is a **security-first vulnerability management platform** that helps teams go from “we have scans” to **clear decisions and fast remediation**.

It is designed for the reality most organizations face: fragmented assets, inconsistent scans, noisy findings, and unclear ownership.

## The real problems KAIZOR solves

### 1) “We don’t know what we own”
Assets are spread across IPs, hostnames, domains, and environments. Ownership changes, documentation is outdated, and the inventory is incomplete.

**KAIZOR helps by** creating a practical, working inventory you can keep updating as you discover assets and ingest scan results.

### 2) “We have thousands of findings, but no prioritization we trust”
Severity alone is not enough. Teams need context: what is exposed, what is business‑critical, what is recurring, and what is already accepted.

**KAIZOR helps by** turning findings into a manageable queue with consistent statuses and a clear view of what matters first.

### 3) “Security findings die in email threads and spreadsheets”
Without a workflow, the same finding gets reopened, duplicates appear, and nobody knows what was fixed or why it was accepted.

**KAIZOR helps by** providing a simple lifecycle for findings and a consistent way to track progress.

### 4) “Reporting is painful and manual”
Most reporting is copy‑paste, incomplete, and hard to repeat.

**KAIZOR helps by** generating a clear “latest report” view that summarizes exposure and highlights top risks.

## What you can do in the Trial

In the 48-hour trial you can:
- Activate and access the product locally
- Add assets (IP/hostname/domain/service)
- Upload scan outputs (e.g., Nessus and Nmap)
- Review findings and change statuses (Open → In Progress → Fixed / Accepted Risk / False Positive)
- Open a “latest report” view for a quick executive summary

## Trial experience and privacy

- The trial runs **locally** on your machine (loopback only).
- It does not require special network setup to get started.

## Who KAIZOR is for

- Security teams who need a practical workflow that doesn’t collapse under noise
- Engineering teams who need clarity on “what to fix first” and “what changed”
- Leadership who needs consistent reporting without manual effort
